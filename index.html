03 — Wages
What Does Work Pay?
Median wages for Black workers compared to all workers, disaggregated by gender — plus an intersectional scorecard and wage gap ratios.
Intersectional Wage Scorecard
Four key wage metrics per state at a glance. Ratio = Black worker median ÷ all worker median (closer to 1.00 = smaller gap). Min wage as % of Black median shows how low the floor sits relative to Black worker earnings.
Median Wage: Black vs. All Workers
Annual median wages by gender, Black workers vs. all workers. Shows absolute earnings gaps.
Black Worker Wages by Gender
Black men vs. Black women — the gender wage gap within the Black workforce.
Wage Gap Ratio — Cents on the Dollar
How many cents does a Black worker earn for every $1 earned by all workers? Lower = larger disparity. 100¢ would mean full parity.

Methodology Notes

"Black" = Black alone, non-Hispanic

Working age = 18–64

Wage figures = full-time workers (35+ hrs/week)

Unemployment from ACS PUMS runs higher than published CPS estimates — figures are valid for cross-state comparison, not external benchmarking
